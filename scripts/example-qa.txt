Q: What are the eligibility requirements for the International Business Awards?
A: The International Business Awards (IBA) is open to organizations worldwide. Any organization, large or small, public or private, for-profit or non-profit, can submit nominations. There are no entry fees for most categories.

Q: What is the deadline for American Business Awards submissions?
A: The American Business Awards typically has submission deadlines in late spring (May/June). Early-bird deadlines offer discounted entry fees. Check the official website for exact dates as they vary by year.

Q: How much does it cost to enter the Stevie Awards?
A: Entry fees vary by program and category. The International Business Awards offers many free categories. The American Business Awards typically charges $350-$450 per entry depending on the deadline. Some categories like Product Awards have different fee structures.

Q: What types of organizations can enter the Stevie Awards?
A: All types of organizations can enter: for-profit companies, non-profit organizations, government agencies, and educational institutions. Categories exist for organizations of all sizes, from startups to Fortune 500 companies.

Q: How are Stevie Awards winners selected?
A: Entries are judged by panels of executives worldwide. Judges score entries on a 0-10 scale across multiple criteria. Gold, Silver, and Bronze Stevie Award winners are determined by average scores. The judging process is blind - judges don't see organization names.
